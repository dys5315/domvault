# 01 — Philosophy

## The split that makes this safe to share
Your second brain is two things wearing one coat:
- the **engine** — structure, conventions, ingestion workflows, the synthesis loop, the agent
  contract. This is *methodology*. Sharing it costs you nothing and helps others.
- the **content** — your actual notes, theses, venture strategy. This is *proprietary*.

Domvault open-sources the engine and never touches the content. The `template/` folder ships
empty. You protect content by **not publishing it**, and you protect the engine with a
**license** that requires attribution and forbids commercial resale.

## Why a "brain", not a wiki
A wiki stores. A brain *connects and generates*. Three properties make it a brain:
1. **Origin tracking** — every note knows who/what authored it (you, ingestion, or the brain
   itself), so the graph can show authorship at a glance.
2. **A synthesis loop** — the system proposes new connections on a schedule. Knowledge
   compounds without manual effort.
3. **Opt-in sharing** — brains can publish nodes to a shared graph, so one person's insight
   can become a building block for another's — with credit intact.

## Why opt-in, not silent ingest
The tempting version of "see everyone's brain" is covert sync. That's spyware: it breaks trust,
it's legally radioactive, and in an open-source repo anyone can read the code and find it. The
durable version is **consensual publishing** — people choose what to share, get credit, and
keep ownership. That's what built GitHub, npm, and Hugging Face. Same mechanics, applied to
knowledge nodes. It's also strictly more valuable: a corpus people *trust* compounds; a corpus
people *fear* gets abandoned.
