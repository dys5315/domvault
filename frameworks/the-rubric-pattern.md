# The Rubric Pattern (Graded, Explainable AI Is the Trust Primitive)

> Intelligence is table stakes; trust is the product. The trust primitive is the rubric: a fixed,
> weighted, explainable scoring scheme graded against ground truth — never raw model output on
> faith.

## The connection
The same design move recurs across otherwise-unrelated AI builds: **define a fixed, weighted,
explainable scoring or verification scheme and grade outputs against a ground truth — never trust
raw model output on faith.** A benchmark that scores generated work across weighted dimensions with
auto-fail triggers; a verifier that grades each claim against its primary source and blocks shipping
on failure; an idea-validator that ranks with a weighted success score *and explains why*; a
synthetic-data harness that grades decisions against known-correct ground truth — they all
independently converge on the same trust mechanism.

## Why it matters
The real product DNA isn't "AI that's smart" — it's "AI whose output is graded, explainable, and
trustworthy." Every flagship artifact converging on the same trust mechanism isn't coincidence;
it's the instinct worth naming. "Graded, auditable AI" is a sharper pitch than "AI-powered," and
it's exactly what high-stakes buyers want: operational reliability over raw cleverness. It's also
harder to copy — anyone can call a model; a transparent grading layer that buyers can audit is the
defensible part.

## So what
- Extract a shared **rubric engine**: define dimensions + weights + ground-truth source + pass/fail
  gates, and reuse it across products. One core, many applications.
- Lead with it: pitch "graded, auditable AI," not "AI-powered."
- Treat the grading layer as the trust surface — make it transparent enough that a skeptical buyer
  can audit the score.
