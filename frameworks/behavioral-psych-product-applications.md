# Behavioral Psychology — Product Applications

> Behavioral psychology is a design input: profile how people actually decide, then build the
> product around the real behavior, not the idealized one.

## Core principles
- **Profile real behavior, not stated intent.** People say one thing and do another; design for the
  observed pattern. Archetype and behavioral profiling beat self-reported preferences.
- **Match the product to the decision the user actually makes.** The same feature lands differently
  depending on the user's archetype, motivation, and context — segment by behavior and tailor the
  surface.
- **Make the desired action the path of least resistance.** Behavioral friction is a lever: reduce
  it where you want action, add it where you want deliberation.

## So what
- Treat behavioral profiling as a first-class input to product and onboarding decisions, not an
  afterthought.
- Where behavior and stated preference disagree, instrument and trust the behavior.
- Use friction deliberately — lower it on the conversion path, raise it where a considered choice
  protects the user.
