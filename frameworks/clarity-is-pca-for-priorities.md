# Clarity Is PCA for Priorities

> "Clarity first, everything else second" is principal component analysis run on your to-do
> space: find the 1–2 axes that explain the most variance in outcomes, and discard the rest as
> noise.

## The connection
A messy mind is a high-dimensional, correlated, noise-laden vector — dozens of tasks, most of them
redundant projections of a few underlying drivers. PCA centers the data, finds the 1–2 axes that
explain the most variance in outcomes, and discards the low-variance directions as noise. That is
exactly what a clarity practice prescribes: capture everything (the raw matrix), cluster and sort
(find correlated features), then ruthlessly project onto the top few daily intentions (the
principal components) and say no to the rest (the discarded dimensions). "If it's not clear, don't
act" is the scree-plot elbow: below the threshold, a dimension isn't worth keeping.

## Why it matters
This turns a soft productivity slogan into a concrete, repeatable operation. It also flags the
trap: PCA finds directions of *maximum spread*, not maximum value — so you still have to define
what "outcome variance" means before projecting, or you optimize the wrong axis. That's the values
step in the clarity map.

## So what
- Weekly drill: list every open priority, ask "which 2 axes explain most of the result this
  week?", cut the rest to a parking lot.
- Define the outcome metric first so PCA projects onto value, not just busyness.
